
* St. Anthony's Sr. Sec. School, Barabanki

  St. Anthony's Sr. Sec. School, also referred to in the uploaded material as St. Anthony's Senior Secondary School, is located at Civil Lines, Court Road, Barabanki, Uttar Pradesh – 225001. It is affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), with CBSE Affiliation Number 2130066 and School Code 70239. The school is managed by the Catholic Diocese of Lucknow and is described as a Recognized but Unaided English Medium Institution and a Christian Minority Co-Educational Institution. Classes offered run from Nursery to Class XII. The medium of instruction is English. Streams mentioned across the sources include Science, Commerce, Computer Education, Computer Science, Mathematics, and Life Sciences. Official website: [www.stanthonyschools.org](http://www.stanthonyschools.org). Email: [stanthonybbk@yahoo.com](mailto:stanthonybbk@yahoo.com). Contact numbers: 05248-222568 and 8174970445. 

* Core Mission / About Us / History

  The school was founded in 1977 with the objective of imparting modern and value-based education. It was established primarily for Catholic Christian students while remaining open to students of all faiths. The institution emphasizes academic excellence, character formation, moral values, discipline, equality, mutual respect, leadership, social responsibility, and holistic development. Its stated educational aim is the integral development of students in the moral, physical, intellectual, social, and cultural dimensions so that they may become worthy and patriotic citizens.

  The institution’s foundational roots go back to 1945, when land for the campus was purchased by the Catholic Diocese of Allahabad. In 1946, the land was transferred to the newly formed Diocese of Lucknow. The first Bishop mentioned is Rt. Rev. Bishop Conard De Vitto OFM Cap. Early development was supported by Fr. Alexander OFM Cap., Fr. Gasper Pinto, Fr. Clement, Fr. Jerome Saldanha, and Fr. Baptist D'Souza.

  In 1977, Fr. Joseph Peter Rodrigues established St. Anthony's School under the vision of Bishop Cecil De Sa. The first classes were conducted in the Priest House, and the school began with approximately 100 students. On 24 November 1979, the foundation stone of the present building was laid by Mr. Naresh Dayal, District Magistrate, Barabanki. On 2 March 1982, the school building was blessed by Rt. Rev. Dr. Cecil De Sa, Bishop of Lucknow. In 1986, the school obtained CBSE affiliation; the first CBSE batch had 18 students and achieved 100% results. The school later expanded to the Senior Secondary level with Science, Mathematics, Life Sciences, and Commerce streams.

  The uploaded material also notes that in the first years the school grew from about 100 students to more than 2,000 students with over 70 staff members. One source says the first four Board-exam batches travelled to Lucknow Central School before the institution became an official CBSE examination center.

  The school patron is St. Antony of Padua. The uploaded text states that he was born in Lisbon, Portugal, in 1195, influenced by St. Francis of Assisi, joined the Franciscan order, worked for the upliftment of the Moors in Africa, died at the age of 36 in 1231, was declared a saint in 1946 by Pope Pius XI (historically written in the institution’s text as Pius Xl), and his feast is celebrated on 13 June. The school associates itself with his spirit of sincerity, honesty, truth, forbearance, justice, and selfless service.

  The school anthem praises the school, the flag colors red, blue, green, and yellow, perseverance, the motto Love, Truth and Knowledge, respect for teachers, and the blessing of the patron saint. The uploaded text also refers to students collectively as “Antoniyans.”

  The school is managed by the Catholic Diocese of Lucknow, described in the source material as a Registered Charitable & Religious Society. The current head of the Diocese is Rt. Rev. Dr. Gerald John Mathias, installed as Bishop of Lucknow on 4 January 2008.

* Infrastructure / Facilities / Rules / Miscellaneous Information

  Campus and general infrastructure:
  The campus area is 7937 square metres.
  Number of classrooms: 38.
  Average classroom size: 46.45 square metres.
  The campus is described as spacious, soundly nurtured, centrally located in Barabanki, with a magnificent main building, a new administrative block at the entrance, lavishly laid playground, well-arranged parking, and lush green ambience.
  Facilities available include well-equipped Science Laboratories, Computer Laboratories, Library, Auditorium, Internet Facility, Smart Learning Environment, Sports Facilities, and Co-Curricular Activity Infrastructure.
  Additional amenities mentioned in the uploaded text include an acoustically engineered auditorium, a multi-utility hall, music room, counselor’s room, a fully equipped multi-bed infirmary, continuous CCTV surveillance, a full-time backup generator, online reports, and SMS alerts for parents regarding performance and attendance.

  Laboratories:
  Computer lab — fully air-conditioned, 60 computers, advanced hardware and upgraded software for multimedia and programming, projector, and interactive board.
  Physics lab — for Classes IX to XII, fully equipped for CBSE AISSCE practical examinations, with apparatus, models, and charts.
  Chemistry lab — well-ventilated, stocked with apparatus, chemicals, salts, and reagents, and with a separate first-aid chamber.
  Biology lab — up to Class XII, spacious for 40 students, equipped with water baths, binocular microscopes, pH meters, sphygmomanometers, an original human skeleton, and biological models.

  Library:
  The library works with the Colon Classification system associated with S. R. Ranganathan.
  It follows an open access system.
  Junior fiction is shelved separately.
  Library hours are given as 8:30 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. in one source.
  Class I to V have class libraries in their own rooms.
  Class VI to X may borrow one book for one week.
  Class XI and XII may borrow two books for one week.

  Admission rules:
  Nursery entry requires the child to be 3½ years old as of 1 April of the admission year.
  Admission is through an online application.
  Required proof includes an official Date of Birth Certificate, accepted from a Municipal Corporation source, hospital source, Family Register, or Baptismal Certificate for Christians.
  A Transfer Certificate is mandatory for children coming from another school.
  If the previous school is outside Uttar Pradesh or is unrecognized, the TC must be countersigned by the District Education Officer.
  Candidates are tested for fitness for the class they seek.
  Admission depends on merit and vacancy.
  Having a sibling in the school does not guarantee admission.
  Guardians must agree to the school handbook, fees, and future rule or fee changes.
  All annual composite fees and examination fees must be paid.
  Class XI admission is not automatic even for existing students.
  Class XI admission depends on board marks, internal assessment, conduct, interview, a signed parental undertaking, and the Principal’s final decision on the required percentage and stream allocation.
  External candidates may enter Class XI only if there is a vacancy and they have exceptional merit.

  Examination and promotion:
  Nursery and K.G. are promoted on periodic assessments.
  For Classes I to XII, the passing mark in each subject is 33%.
  Attendance below 75% requires a written explanation and depends on the Principal’s discretion.
  Students absent from an exam cannot simply retake it later.
  If a student is ill, a medical certificate must be submitted within 3 days.
  Missing a paper disqualifies the student from merit ranking.
  Fees must be paid before examinations begin; otherwise students may be barred from the exam hall or their results withheld.
  If a student repeats the same class for two successive years, the student is asked to withdraw and issued a Transfer Certificate.
  The Principal’s decision is final in doubtful promotion cases.
  One source adds that answer scripts are not retained for more than 3 months after the examination.

  Disciplinary rules:
  Students must be respectful and polite, maintain cleanliness, wear proper uniform, arrive at least 10 minutes before time, maintain silence during assemblies and academic activities, speak English on school premises, and participate in sports and co-curricular activities.
  Prohibited items include mobile phones, electronic gadgets, firecrackers, explosives, sharp instruments, and unauthorized books, magazines, newspapers, comics, or periodicals.
  Private tuition from school staff requires the Principal’s permission.
  Coaching-centre attendance must not interfere with school activities.
  Damage to school property must be paid for or repaired by the responsible student.
  Students must not write, carve, or scratch on walls or furniture.
  Unapproved collections, fines, meetings, or picnics are not allowed.
  The school discourages parallel education and treats prioritizing coaching over school requirements as a serious offense.
  Parents must immediately report infectious disease in the home, and affected students should stay away until the danger of contagion has passed.
  The school accepts no liability for lost valuables, money, clothes, or ornaments.
  Bicycles may be parked in the designated area at the owner’s risk and must be locked.
  Cars and motorized scooters are banned.
  Parking two-wheelers near the school gate is prohibited.
